Scholarship Application Process

The Sri Lanka Student Empowerment Foundation (SLSEF) follows a transparent and fair selection process to ensure that scholarships are awarded to deserving students with genuine financial needs. The following steps outline the complete process from application to award.

1. Application Collection

Applications are collected from a selected set of schools across Sri Lanka. Eligible students can apply by completing the official SLSEF Scholarship Application Form, available through participating schools or our online platform.

2. Initial Screening

After reviewing all applications, a shortlist of candidates will be prepared based on financial need and academic merit. Shortlisted students will be contacted and asked to provide supporting documents to verify their financial background.

Unless there is a strong reason not to award the scholarship, all shortlisted candidates who complete the next steps successfully will be granted the scholarship.

3. Collecting Additional Documents

Shortlisted students must submit the following documents:

  • A letter certified by the Grama Niladhari Officer confirming the applicant’s financial situation.
  • A certified copy of the official government-issued O/L certificate.
  • (Optional) For current A/L students — a letter from the school confirming results from the last term’s exams will be useful.

4. Interview

Selected students will be invited for a short interview conducted remotely via a video communication platform such as Zoom. This step helps us understand the applicant’s goals, motivation, and circumstances better.

5. Scholarships Are Awarded

Students who successfully complete all previous steps and meet the eligibility criteria will be officially awarded the SLSEF scholarship. Each recipient will receive LKR 5,000 per month for a period of one year.

Based on performance and continued financial need, scholarships may be extended for an additional year.

6. Midterm Check-in

Six months after the award, SLSEF conducts a midterm progress check with each student. This check ensures that recipients are continuing their studies successfully and maintaining good progress.

In rare cases, a scholarship may be discontinued due to unsatisfactory progress or failure to attend the midterm check-in.
