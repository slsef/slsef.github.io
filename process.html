Selection process

A clear, fair path from application to award.

SLSEF uses a transparent review process so students understand what happens after they submit the form and why each step matters.

What to expect

The process moves from application review to document checks, interview, award, and follow-up.

Financial need and academic commitment remain central throughout the selection.

Shortlisted applicants are guided on what to submit next so there are no hidden steps.

How the process works

These are the six core stages that applicants can expect from initial submission through the first progress review.

1

Application Collection

Applications are collected from selected schools across Sri Lanka and through the official online form.

2

Initial Screening

Applications are reviewed for financial need and academic merit. Shortlisted students are contacted for the next stage.

Unless there is a strong reason not to award the scholarship, shortlisted candidates who complete the required steps successfully will receive support.

3

Additional Documents

Shortlisted students are asked to verify their background with supporting documents.

  • A letter certified by the Grama Niladhari Officer confirming the applicant’s financial situation.
  • A certified copy of the official government-issued O/L certificate.
  • Optional: a letter from the school confirming results from the last term’s exams.
4

Interview

Selected students join a short remote interview, usually through a platform such as Zoom, so SLSEF can better understand their goals and circumstances.

5

Scholarship Award

Successful applicants are awarded the scholarship and receive LKR 5,000 per month for one year.

Based on continued need and performance, support may be extended for an additional year.

6

Midterm Check-in

Six months after the award, SLSEF checks each student’s progress to confirm that studies are continuing well.

In rare cases, support may be discontinued if progress is unsatisfactory or the student does not attend the check-in.

Prepare these items early

Having the key details ready before you apply makes the process smoother and reduces delays after shortlisting.

  • Your personal and family financial details.
  • Your latest academic records.
  • Supporting documents that verify financial need.
  • A short explanation of your educational goals and circumstances.
